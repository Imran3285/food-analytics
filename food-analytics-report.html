Key findings
Delivery physics

Distance is the dominant ETA driver (r = 0.86)

Distance–time correlation of 0.86 is the strongest signal in the dataset. Every additional kilometre adds ~2.5 min to delivery time. Weather adds a further 12–15 min under severe conditions.

Partner performance

53% efficiency uplift over a partner career

Efficiency grows from 46.7 at year 1 to 71.6 at year 15 — a consistent +1.8 pts/year experience curve. Partner retention is a strategic lever that routing optimisation alone cannot replicate.

Economics

Premium customers pay more, receive identical SLAs

Premium mean order value ≈ ₹124 vs standard ₹110 (+13%), yet delivery time and delay rates are indistinguishable between segments — an unmonetised service-level opportunity.

Loyalty programme

Bronze to Diamond spend is flat at ₹113–114

Order value shows no differentiation across all five loyalty tiers. The programme creates zero spend incentive — a structural design failure requiring fundamental redesign.

Operational risk

27% of orders impacted by operational events

Of 15,000 total orders: 2,003 cancelled (13.4%), 1,420 delayed (9.5%), 618 refunded (4.1%). Only 10,959 orders are fully clean. Severe weather spikes delay rates to 10.4%.

Rating quality

Ratings locked at 4.0 — systemic inflation

Customer ratings cluster at 4.0 across all segments, age groups, and loyalty tiers. This is a measurement problem, not a satisfaction signal. The rating UX incentivises generous responses.

Feature engineering

Eight derived features were created to enable deeper analysis beyond the raw columns:

delivery_delay_minutes
delivery_time − estimated_delivery_time · quantifies under/over-performance vs ETA
revenue_per_km
final_amount_paid / delivery_distance_km · unit economics normalised by distance
tip_rate
tip_amount / order_value · normalised generosity signal independent of order size
discount_rate
discount_amount / order_value · effective discount depth per order
age_group
Binned: 18–25 · 26–35 · 36–45 · 46–55 · 56–65 · customer lifecycle segmentation
weather_bucket
5-bin cut on weather_severity_score · categorical weather severity for group analysis
loyalty_tier
Bronze / Silver / Gold / Platinum / Diamond · human-readable loyalty segmentation
exp_bucket
1–3 · 4–6 · 7–10 · 11–15 yrs · partner career stage grouping

Business implications
Distance is the #1 delivery time driver
Prioritise hyperlocal restaurant onboarding in dense delivery zones. Each kilometre reduction in average distance saves ~2.5 min per order.
53% efficiency gap between new and experienced partners
Invest in partner retention. Build structured mentorship and incentive programmes for high-experience partners — the efficiency curve compounds over time.
Premium customers receive no faster service
Introduce SLA-based priority dispatch for premium tier. Quantify willingness-to-pay for guaranteed ETA to size the revenue opportunity.
~27% of orders impacted by cancellations, delays, or refunds
Build a pre-dispatch risk scoring model using distance, weather, time-of-day, and partner experience as features to flag at-risk orders before dispatch.
Rating inflation — 4.0 is universal across every segment
Redesign rating UX to include multi-dimensional feedback (food quality, packaging, punctuality) with friction on high ratings to reduce inflation bias.
Loyalty tier spending is completely undifferentiated
Redesign loyalty programme with spend-linked thresholds and exclusive perks for Platinum/Diamond. A/B test spend uplift vs control group before full rollout.
Severe weather spikes delays by 12–15 min
Surface weather-adjusted ETA buffers proactively in the customer-facing app before the order is placed, not after the delay occurs.

Reproducing the analysis
Option A — Kaggle (recommended, zero setup)

Open the notebook directly on Kaggle and add the dataset: deepeshkansotia/food-delivery-operations-and-customer-analytics

Option B — Local

git clone https://github.com/Imran3285/food-analytics.git
cd food-analytics

# Install dependencies
pip install pandas numpy matplotlib seaborn scipy kagglehub

# Place Kaggle API credentials
# ~/.kaggle/kaggle.json (Linux/Mac) or %USERPROFILE%\.kaggle\kaggle.json (Windows)

# Launch notebook
jupyter notebook food-delivery-operations-customer-analytic-eda.ipynb
